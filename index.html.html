Four Cost Components

Direct Legal Cost captures internal lawyer time, amplified by a context-switching multiplier that accounts for the productivity loss from stop-start work across multiple contracts. Outside Counsel Cost represents external legal spend, with only the portion affected by cycle time (e.g., scope creep from prolonged negotiations) factored into the reduction. Sales Carrying Cost quantifies the opportunity cost of BD/sales team time spent in limbo during legal review. Working Capital Cost measures the financing cost of capital tied up in pending deals.

Legal Leverage Ratio

This ratio (legal review time ÷ total cycle time) shows what percentage of the deal lifecycle is legal-dependent. It prevents overclaiming — if legal touches 40% of the cycle, a 50% legal improvement yields ~20% overall improvement, not 50%.

Reduction Assumptions

Touchpoints reduce at 60% of the rate of time reduction (fewer handoffs, but not eliminated). The context-switching multiplier reduces proportionally but with dampening. BD carrying cost reduces at 50% of the legal reduction rate (sales time improves but not linearly with legal speed). Revenue Acceleration Value uses a 40% gross margin assumption.

What This Does Not Account For

Win rate decay (deals dying due to delays), competitive displacement, employee morale, and second-order effects on client relationships. The actual impact is likely higher than the figures shown.
